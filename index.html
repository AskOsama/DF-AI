Digital Forensics Data & Artificial Intelligence

A five-part field guide to the data digital forensics works with — where it comes from, who owns it, how to obtain and handle it lawfully and admissibly — and how to build your own in-house AI tools and agents to analyse it, without handing the evidence (or yourself) to outside services.

🌍 Bilingual (EN / عربي) 🧭 Five parts · medium depth 🔬 Forensics × AI

One arc, told in five parts. Part 1 sets the vocabulary — data science is the field that studies data, AI is the set of algorithms that handle it, and digital forensics is the kind of data we must analyse. Part 2 follows the data itself: where it is generated, where it lives, how volatile it is, who owns it, and how you obtain it from other parties. Part 3 draws the legal and ethical lines — admissibility, the privacy policies you must not violate, your own operational security, and how "anonymous" data is re-identified. Part 4 opens the toolbox: AI is far more than machine learning — constraint solving, optimization, knowledge reasoning, topic modelling and search are all tools you can run in-house. Part 5 wires those tools into an agent.

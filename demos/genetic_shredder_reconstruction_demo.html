Algorithm: Greedy raster-fill · Genetic algorithm

Greedy places each tile once and never reconsiders, so on a fine grid one wrong tile cascades. A genetic algorithm keeps a whole population of candidate arrangements and evolves them — selection, crossover, mutation, plus a little local refinement (memetic) — generation after generation, keeping the best. Watch the best edge cost fall and the picture assemble.

Reconstruction by edge matching only works when tiles are distinctive. On the content-rich image the GA reaches near-perfect at a modest grid; on the document page it struggles — blank whitespace makes many tiles interchangeable, so even the optimal-cost arrangement isn't the true one (that's the honest limit, and why the strip-cut demo works but cross-cut text is hard). Fine grids are harder for the generic operators — raise the grid to watch accuracy fall (scalability). Uses the Gallagher prediction-based edge dissimilarity.

You were right that the fitness/objective is the lever — a GA only ever finds what you tell it to optimise. Use the Fitness / objective selector to experiment: same GA, same real shredded PDF page, different objective (naive edge-cost sum vs composition-aware best-buddy agreement). Watch the objective fall while accuracy stays low — the gap between "lowest number" and "right composition" is the lesson.

The honest result (I tested this hard): on this page no edge-based objective recovers the cross-cut composition — switching the objective changes which wrong page you get, but not the accuracy (all land low). The binding constraint here is the data: a downscaled, posterized text render is mostly whitespace with repeating text rows, so many tiles are near-interchangeable — the true page isn't even the objective's minimum. The objective's power shows where pieces are distinctive: the strip-cut demo reaches 96% (full-height strips), and greedy from a corner recovers this page at a coarse grid.
